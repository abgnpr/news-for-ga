### PM Modi Inaugurates Kaynes Semicon OSAT Facility at Sanand — 60 Lakh Chips Per Day at Full Capacity

Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Kaynes Semicon's outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) facility at Sanand, Gujarat on March 31 — the third semiconductor-related inauguration at the fast-emerging industrial cluster near Ahmedabad. The ₹3,300-crore facility was approved by the Union Cabinet in September 2024 under the India Semiconductor Mission (ISM) and has an estimated production capacity of approximately 60 lakh chips per day once fully operational.

**Perspective & Context:**
- *In simple terms:* An OSAT facility takes semiconductor wafers fabricated elsewhere, then packages, assembles, and tests them into finished chips ready to go into phones, cars, and appliances. Kaynes Semicon's Sanand plant is part of India's push to build domestic chip manufacturing capability and reduce dependence on foreign supply chains — a lesson sharpened by the global chip shortage of 2020–22.
- **OSAT (Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Test)** — The back-end stage of chip production: cutting wafers into individual dies, packaging them (bonding to substrates, encapsulating in resin), and testing for defects. Distinct from front-end wafer fabrication, which requires far more advanced and expensive technology. Most global OSAT activity is concentrated in Taiwan, South Korea, and China.
- **India Semiconductor Mission (ISM)** — A government initiative under MeitY offering fiscal incentives (up to 50% of project cost) for semiconductor and display manufacturing units. Designed to build India's position across the chip value chain — from OSAT and compound semiconductors to, eventually, advanced logic fabs.
- Three semiconductor inaugurations at Sanand in quick succession marks a tangible acceleration of ISM's implementation — the Sanand–Ahmedabad corridor is emerging as India's chip manufacturing cluster, with proximity to Gujarat's automotive and electronics manufacturing base being a key advantage.
- 60 lakh chips per day (~2.2 billion per year at full capacity) is a significant output for India's domestic supply chain — enough to meaningfully cover requirements for automotive chips, IoT devices, and consumer electronics that India currently imports almost entirely.
